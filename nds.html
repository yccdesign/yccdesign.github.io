PROJECT 1: NOW-TAB

Addition of a small variant for now-tab


Many components in the Now Design System (NDS) have a small size variant, but the tab component only had a medium size. For products using small-sized components to present denser information in their UI, the lack of a smaller-sized tab variant has become a challenge for multiple product teams attempting to create a consistent experience.

Instead of request for the design system team to include a new size variant for the tab component in a future release, I initiated the project to contribute a smaller size variant directly to the design system to achieve a more unified look for my product teams.

Over the 4 months, I've coordinated with the designer, PM and engineers from the NDS team to ensure project progress aligned with the release timeline for our own products and updated usage guidelines nad Figma libary. I've also worked closely with a developer from our team to ensure the built component matched the visual specs.

This was one of the first few projects where I am not only the designer but also the lead. I've learned about the complex cross-functional considerations and alignment necessary to contribute to a large design system, where designing and coding actually took the least time. This experience has given me the confidence to initiate similar projects to overcome product design constraints.

PROJECT 2: NDS ICONS

Revising 11 icons for the new Now-icon design language


As an effort to revamp the NDS icon library, I transformed 11 new icons originally createded by a visual designer to match with the new design aesthetics on Figma.

This was my first contribution to the NDS icon library. Throughout the review and re-iteration process, I learned a lot about different considerations with regards to icon designs to achieve pixel=-perfect outcomes, such as pixel-alignment for complicated vectors and internationalization concerns.
